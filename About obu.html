Oda Bultum University Profile

Discover the academic excellence and institutional structure of one of Ethiopia's premier higher education institutions

University Establishment

Oda Bultum University was established in 2015 E.C. (2022/23 G.C.) as part of Ethiopia's ambitious higher education expansion program. Located in Chiro, West Hararghe Zone of Oromia Regional State, the university was founded to serve as a center of excellence for education, research, and community service in eastern Ethiopia.

The university is named after the renowned Oda Bultum, a historical figure who played significant roles in the resistance against foreign occupation. This namesake reflects the institution's commitment to cultivating leadership and preserving cultural heritage.

Key establishment facts:

  • Founded: 2015 E.C. (2022/23 G.C.)
  • Location: Chiro, West Hararghe Zone, Oromia
  • Initial student intake: 1,200 students
  • First programs: 8 undergraduate programs
  • Current student population: Over 5,000

Colleges & Schools

Oda Bultum University is organized into several colleges and schools, each focusing on specific academic disciplines while promoting interdisciplinary collaboration. This structure enables comprehensive education and specialized research across diverse fields.

The university's academic structure reflects Ethiopia's development priorities while maintaining international academic standards. Each college is led by a dean and has its own academic and administrative offices.

Current colleges include:

  • College of Natural and Computational Sciences
  • College of Social Sciences and Humanities
  • College of Business and Economics
  • College of Agriculture and Environmental Sciences
  • College of Health Sciences
  • College of Engineering and Technology
  • School of Law
  • School of Education and Behavioral Sciences

Academic Departments

Oda Bultum University houses numerous academic departments that deliver specialized programs aligned with national development goals and global academic standards. These departments serve as the primary academic units responsible for teaching, research, and community engagement.

The university continuously evaluates and expands its departments to meet emerging needs in Ethiopia's job market and research landscape.
